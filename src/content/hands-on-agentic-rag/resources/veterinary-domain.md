- Source: Series domain decisions, AVMA pet care resources, and AAHA pet owner resources.

- What not to use this for: clinical facts, treatment guidance, diagnostic claims, dosage, or veterinary medical advice.

## Domain Vocabulary

People and roles:

- veterinarian;
- clinic team;
- receptionist;
- technician;
- tutor or owner.

Animals and records:

- pet;
- species;
- breed;
- age;
- weight;
- vaccination record;
- consultation history;
- lab report;
- prescription;
- medication list;
- feeding notes;
- clinical event;
- follow-up.

Common non-diagnostic event examples:

- vomiting;
- diarrhea;
- apathy;
- reduced appetite;
- pain sign observed by tutor;
- feeding change;
- medication change;
- weight change;
- vaccine visit.

## Safe Phrasing

Use:

- "observed signs";
- "events recorded by the tutor";
- "facts found in the documents";
- "points to discuss with the veterinarian";
- "warning signs that should prompt urgent veterinary care";
- "the documents are insufficient to confirm this";
- "the agent cannot diagnose or prescribe."

Avoid:

- "the pet has [diagnosis]";
- "give this medication";
- "change the dosage";
- "this is not urgent" when evidence is insufficient;
- "you do not need a veterinarian."

## Sample Data Ideas

Use fictional pets and clinics. Keep examples realistic but non-clinical:

- Luna, cat, vaccination card and weight records.
- Bento, dog, feeding change followed by vomiting notes.
- Kassandra, cat, scanned lab report and consultation history.
- Internal clinic team member preparing a consultation briefing.

## Series Rules

- Use veterinary examples to explain AI engineering concepts.
- Do not teach veterinary medicine.
- When discussing urgent situations, phrase as escalation to professional care.
- Verify external veterinary sources before writing posts with general education content.
- Keep all examples fictional.
